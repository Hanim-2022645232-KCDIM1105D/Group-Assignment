Temperature and humidity are both inversely proportional. If the temperature increases, the air will become drier and the humidity will decrease. Inappropriate levels of either temperature or humidity will affect the material condition.

  • Paper documents are stored at 2–18 °C and 30–50% relative humidity.
  • Electronic records are stored at 11°C and 30% relative humidity.

Light is one of the most common causes of damage to records. Materials like paper and photographs can all be harmed by sunlight and constant interior lighting. It will lead to color fading or darkening. We will protect them by boxing them, sleeving them, or covering them when not in use.
